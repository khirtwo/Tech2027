Bantay Bell Tower Cape Bajeador Lighthouse Mayon Volcano Kayangan Lake Banaune Rice Terraces
The Bantay Bell Tower (often called Bantay Belfry) is a historic, red-brick watchtower situated atop a lush hill in Bantay, Ilocos Sur. Located just 10 minutes northeast of Vigan City, it serves as the belfry for the adjacent St. Augustine Parish Church. The Cape Bojeador Lighthouse, also known as the Burgos Lighthouse, is a historic Spanish-era structure located in the municipality of Burgos, Ilocos Norte. Set high atop the Vigia de Nagpartian Hill, it sits 160 meters above sea level and offers sweeping panoramic views of the South China Sea. Mayon Volcano is an active stratovolcano located in Albay province, southeastern Luzon, Philippines, approximately 325 kilometers southeast of Manila. It is celebrated for its near-perfect, symmetrical conical shape, rising to an elevation of 2,462 meters (8,077 feet) above the Albay Gulf. Kayangan Lake is located on Coron Island in Palawan, Philippines. Often cited as the cleanest lake in the country, it is surrounded by dramatic karst limestone cliffs and features crystal-clear, emerald-green, brackish waters and otherworldly underwater rock formations. The Banaue Rice Terraces are a 2,000-year-old system of irrigated paddies carved into the mountains of Banaue, Ifugao, in the Philippines. Located about 1,500 meters above sea level, these hand-carved agricultural marvels are fed by an ancient irrigation system originating from the mountaintop rainforests

VISAYAS

               
Simala Shrine in Sibonga Sirao Flower Garden Kawasan Falls Chocolate Hills Kalanggaman Island
The Simala Shrine, officially known as the Monastery of the Holy Eucharist, is located in Barangay Lindogon, Sibonga, Cebu. It sits roughly 54–60 kilometers south of Cebu City, a journey that typically takes 2 to 3 hours by bus, car, or van. Sirao Flower Garden (also known as "Little Amsterdam") is an 8,000-square-meter uphill floral farm in Barangay Sirao, Cebu City. Located about 17 km (a 30–45 minute drive) from the city center via the Transcentral Highway, it is famed for its vibrant blooms, whimsical Amsterdam-themed replicas, and Instagram-worthy backdrops. Located in Barangay Matutinao, Badian, on the southwestern coast of Cebu Island, Kawasan Falls is a famous three-tiered waterfall system renowned for its striking, crystal-clear turquoise waters. Fed by the Kabukalan Spring, its ice-cold, refreshing water flows into the Matutinao River. The Chocolate Hills are a unique geological formation on Bohol Island in the Philippines. This natural wonder consists of over 1,200 to 1,776 almost symmetrical, cone-shaped hills that spread over an area of more than 50 square kilometers. During the dry season, the green grass covering the limestone mounds dries up and turns brown, giving them the appearance of giant chocolate mounds. Kalanggaman Island is a stunning, uninhabited island located in the Visayas region of the Philippines, situated in the sea between Cebu and Leyte. It sits approximately 10 kilometers west of the municipality of Palompon, Leyte, and about a 1.5 to 2-hour boat ride from Malapascua Island in Cebu.

MINDANAO

               
Sohoton Cove Maria Cristiana Falls Asik-Asik Falls Anahawon Ridge
Sunken Cemetery
Sohoton Cove is a mystical sanctuary in Socorro, Surigao del Norte, situated on the southwestern tip of Bucas Grande Island. Accessible by a 90 to 120-minute boat ride from Siargao, it is a secluded network of emerald lagoons, towering limestone cliffs, and bioluminescent caves. Located in the City of Majestic Waterfalls, Maria Cristina Falls lies 9.3 km southwest of the Iligan City proper, sitting at the boundaries of Barangays Maria Cristina, Ditucalan, and Buru-un. The 98-meter (321.5-foot) waterfall features a distinct rock at its brink that splits the river, creating a striking "twin falls" effect. Asik-Asik Falls is a majestic curtain-like spring waterfall in Sitio Dulao, Barangay Upper Dado, Alamada, North Cotabato. It stretches 140 meters wide, with 60-meter-high fresh spring water cascading from the moss-covered cliff rather than a river source. It is accessed via a scenic motorbike ride and a trek down roughly 350+ stone steps.The remote destination offers a unique visual spectacle and a refreshing, cold-water hydro-massage. Because there is no deep catch basin at the source, visitors can safely walk up to the cascading walls. Anahawon Ridge (also known as the Valencia City Skypark) is located in Sitio Sumpong, Brgy. Banlag, Valencia City, Bukidnon, Philippines.It is a rising eco-tourism destination offering a "sky park" atmosphere. The ridge is highly celebrated for its breathtaking panoramic vistas, fresh mountain air, and opportunities to see the famous "sea of clouds" during sunrise. It is also equipped with a Glass Walk View Deck that juts out over the rolling hills, providing an immersive experience over the landscape. The Sunken Cemetery is located in Catarman, Camiguin Island, Philippines. It is an underwater graveyard that sank beneath the sea following the eruptions of Mount Vulcan between 1871 and the 1950s. The site is famously marked by a giant, white concrete cross rising above the water, which serves as both a memorial and a popular snorkeling and scuba diving destination.
